Heat Transfer
Heat can be transferred from one place to another by three
methods: conduction in solids, convection of fluids (liquids
or gases), and radiation through anything that will allow
radiation to pass. Heating systems use a convection transfer
method to transfer heat.
Hydronic Heating Systems
Hydronic heating systems rely on circulating hot water
to deliver heat to the locations where heating is desired.
Instead of using large air ducts, such as furnaces, water
is heated at one location and then pumped to other
places via piping. The heat is transferred to the space by
heat exchangers located within the space. These heat
exchangers are usually radiators or baseboards and are also
called terminal units.
Hydronic Terminal Units
