Gas Water Heaters:
Cleaning Gas Burners and Replacing the Thermocouple
(continued)
How-to-Steps
1. Turn off the gas to the water heater by turning the control valve to OFF. Follow Lockout/Tagout procedures to lock the
gas valve.
2. Wait at least ten minutes for the gas to dissipate.
3. Unscrew the gas tube for the pilot light and the burner gas tube from the control box.
4. Unscrew the thermocouple from the control box with a wrench.
5. Remove the burner door.
6. Free the pilot gas tube, the burner gas tube, and the thermocouple wire from the control box by pulling down on
them slightly.
7. Remove the burner and unscrew it from the burner gas tube (if possible).
8. Brush debris from burner ports with a toothbrush or small soft bristle brush. Clean the pilot gas tube with a piece of wire.
9. Screw the burner back on to the gas tube (if it was removed).
10. Slide out old thermocouple and push in new thermocouple.
11. Insert the burner unit into burner door.
12. Screw the thermocouple and both gas tube into the control box.
13. Turn on the gas, check for leaks, and relight the pilot light.
14. Make sure the pilot flame wraps around the tip of the thermocouple. If needed, adjust the thermocouple with
needlenose pliers until the tip is in the flame.
15. Replace the burner door.
