Repairing a Compression Faucet
Older apartment buildings may have compression faucets, which use washers. If you
have a leaking compression faucet, replace the washers and O-rings. You may also need
to replace the valve seats.
Safety
Tools and Materials Needed
Be careful using a utility knife.
  • Screwdriver
  • Handle-puller (if needed)
  • Tongue and groove pliers
  • Utility knife
  • Seat wrench or seat-dressing
    • tool (if needed)
  • Universal washer kit
  • Heatproof grease
  • Replacement valve seats
    • (if needed)
    How-to Steps
    1. Shut off the water to the faucet.
    2. Unscrew the screw that holds the handle in place.
    3. Pull off the handle.
    4. Using a tongue and grove pliers, unscrew (counterclockwise) the stem unit. If the stem unit is worn, replace the faucet.
    5. Examine the valve seat for wear. Run your finger around the rim of the seat, and if it feels rough, replace it. (See “How to
    Replace Valve Scat” on page 34.)
    6. Using a screwdriver, disassemble the stem unit and remove the worn stem washer.
    7. Unscrew the threaded spindle from the retaining nut
    8. Cover new O-ring with grease.
    9. Remove old O-ring and replace with new greased O-ring.
    10. Replace washer and stem screw.
    11. Grease stem unit.
    12. Reassemble the faucet.
    13. Turn on water and check for leaks.
