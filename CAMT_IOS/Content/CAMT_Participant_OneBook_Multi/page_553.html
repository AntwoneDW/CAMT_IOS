Protection from Workplace Hazards
14.
What is a physical hazard and what are some potentially hazardous materials?
  • A physical hazard is an object that could cause injury, illness, or death
    • through external contact with the body and can include:
    o sharp tools
    o heavy pieces of equipment
    o metal edges on appliance or equipment
    o slippery floors
  • A potentially hazardous material is a substance that could possibly cause
    • injury, illness, or death by entering the body and can include:
    o electricity
    o body fluids (such as blood and other bodily substances)
    o chemicals (such as cleaning products, paints, and pesticides)
    o gases and airborne particles of matter (such as refrigerants,
    chlorine gas, and asbestos)
    15.
    What is Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)?
  • PPE refers to special clothing and equipment that people wear to protect
    • them from workplace hazards and protects from both physical hazards and
    potentially hazardous materials.
  • PPE includes wearing proper clothing to protect arms, legs, feet and body –
    • without catching on an object or piece of equipment, little or no jewelry
    (including chains, earrings, and rings) to avoid getting caught on something or
    conducting electricity.
