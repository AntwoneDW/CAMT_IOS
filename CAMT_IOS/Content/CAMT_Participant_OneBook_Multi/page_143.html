Plumbing in an Apartment Building
The plumbing in an apartment building is a gravity/pressure-based system that is made
up of three parts: water supply, water distribution, and waste exit.
Water Supply
Water is distributed throughout the city in large underground pipes and makes its way to houses, businesses, and
apartments. Water meters track the amount of water used at each location.
Water Distribution
Pipes inside the apartment building carry pressurized cold and hot water to faucets, showers, toilets, and appliances in
each apartment.
Waste Exit
Waste water and sewage go out drains and toilets. They are piped out of the apartment and make their way to a sewage
treatment plant
  • Vents deliver fresh air to the drains so the waste can flow freely.
  • U-shaped pipes called traps at each drain keep sewer gasses from entering the building.
    • The waste exit system is also called the DWV or drain, waste, and vent system.
    Plumbing Terms
    Pipes: Pipes are metal or plastic tubes that carry water, sewage, and fresh air to the plumbing system.
    Fittings: Fittings are metal or plastic pieces that connect straight sections of pipe together. Typical fittings include
    elbows and Ts.
    Valves: Valves are pieces of equipment that control the flow of water in a plumbing system and include faucets
    and shut-offs.
    Fixtures: Fixtures are areas in an apartment where water is used, such as sinks, showers, bathtubs, and toilets.
    Appliances: Appliances are machines that use water, such as water heaters, dishwashers, washing machines, water
    softeners, and garbage disposals.
