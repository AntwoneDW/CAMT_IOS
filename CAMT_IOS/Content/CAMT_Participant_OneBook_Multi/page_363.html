Load Circuits: Fan Motors
In this part of the training, you’ll learn how to troubleshoot the fan motor, particularly the
capacitors and motor windings associated with it.
The good news is that these troubleshooting steps are nearly identical to those for
compressor motors.
Introduction
Fan motors (both the indoor and outdoor varieties) are another one of the key electrical loads used in cooling systems.
Much like the compressor motor, you want to test the fan motor if it doesn’t seem to be running properly. Again, you want to
be completely certain that a fan motor is bad before you replace it.
What You’ll Learn
Once again, our training will concentrate on the more common electrical (or load circuit) problems associated with fan motors.
These include:
  • Bad capacitors
  • Open, shorted, or grounded motor windings
    • You’ll learn how to test for all of these conditions, and even better, you’ll find them nearly identical to the tests you just learned
    for the compressor motor.
    For More Information
    If you’d like to learn more about the mechanical failures that can occur in a fan motor, take some time outside of class to read
    the motor information in Section 4 of the HVAC Servicing Procedures manual.
