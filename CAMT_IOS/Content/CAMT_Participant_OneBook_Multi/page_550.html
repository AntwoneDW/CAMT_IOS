Certificate for Apartment Maintenance Technicians (CAMT) Skill Checks with Answer Key
Inside the Apartment Business
Skill Check #7—Answer Key
You are Here: Maintaining the Community—Requested Maintenance
1. Who is responsible for curb appeal?
  • All members of the apartment community team including property
    • managers, leasing consultants, and maintenance technicians.
    2. What does curb appeal include?
  • Curb appeal includes the general appearance and condition of:
    • o Signs and other advertising
    o Landscaping and grounds, fencing and lighting
    o Driveways, streets, parking lots, sidewalks, and breezeways
    o Building interiors, exteriors, and hallways
    o Amenities such as swimming pools and laundry rooms
    o Offices, other common use areas, model apartments, and
    vacant apartments
    3. How do you ensure that the apartment community has a good appearance?
    • Find out if the community has a curb appeal or a grounds checklist, and if
    it does, make sure to use it.
    4. What happens if a resident asks for maintenance as a personal favor?
    • Most apartment community or management companies have a policy on
    whether or not this is allowed. Review this policy before proceeding. If
    personal favors are allowed, be sure to keep a record of your work.
    5. Why are service requests used?
    • To maintain a written record of all technical service that is performed for
    the following reasons.
    o Record how residents’ needs were met and when they were met.
    o To track potential or actual maintenance problems.
    o To guide preventive maintenance efforts.
    o To guide budget planning and implementation for the community.
    o To demonstrate that the apartment community team has not
    discriminated in providing service to residents. Discrimination may
    be evidenced by delayed response or incomplete response.
    o To document and verify that they had permission to enter the unit.
