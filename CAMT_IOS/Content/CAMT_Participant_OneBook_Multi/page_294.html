Key Parts of Hydronic Heating Systems (continued)
Pressure-Relief Valve
This valve opens if the pressure in the system reaches the set point on the valve. When the pressure drops to an acceptable
level, the valve will close. Building codes require that a pressure relief valve is installed on every hot water boiler.
Zone Valve/Dial Valve (Dan Floss Dials)
Zone valves open and close in response to the temperature in the coordinating space. When a zone calls for heat, the zone
valve opens and trips the switch that starts the circulator. The circulator runs until the thermostat is satisfied.
Flow Check Valve
This valve prevents hot water from flowing through a heating loop when no heat is desired in that space.
Balancing Valve
This valve helps keep an even water flow through each branch of the circuit.
