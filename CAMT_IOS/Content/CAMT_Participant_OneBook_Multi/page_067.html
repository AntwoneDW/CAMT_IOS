Electrical Safety (Continued)
Key Safety Rule #1:
Turn Power Off
Always turn off power to the area and to the electrical
equipment. Then use a circuit tester (or other electrical
meter) to make sure the power is off . Never take
chances. Verify everything.
Safety Rule #2:
Follow Lockout/Tagout Procedures
Lockout/tagout is a safety procedure to make sure that
power and energy sources, such as electricity, are shut
off and secured properly.
You’ll use lockout/tagout procedures when doing electrical
maintenance and repairs, such as installing new switches,
receptacles, and lighting fixtures—or investigating a
short circuit.
Lockout Devices and Tags
Typical lockout devices that you’ll use include:
  • Padlocks
  • Gate and ball valve lockouts
  • Circuit breaker lockouts
  • Chains
  • Cable
    • Lockout tags usually are brightly colored so you can
    immediately see and identify them.
    Your company may have lockout/tagout kits they want you
    to use. Check with your supervisor.
