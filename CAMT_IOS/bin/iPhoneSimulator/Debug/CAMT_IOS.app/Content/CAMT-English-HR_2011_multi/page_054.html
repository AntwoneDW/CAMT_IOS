Pressure-Reducing Valve
A valve that automatically drops the
pressure of the water to the level at
which the boiler was designed to
operate. If the pressure drops below
a certain level, the valve opens to
give the boiler more water.

Pressure-Relief Valve
A safety valve that opens if the
pressure in the system reaches
the set point on the valve.
When the pressure drops to an
acceptable level, the valve will
close. Building codes require that
a pressure relief valve is installed
Pressure-Relief Valve
on every hot water boiler.
Sequencer
The sequencer energizes the
blower motor and the multiple
heating elements at regular time
intervals by opening and closing
electrical contacts.
