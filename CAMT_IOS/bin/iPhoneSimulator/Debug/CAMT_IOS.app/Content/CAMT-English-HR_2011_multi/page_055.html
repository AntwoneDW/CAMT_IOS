Standard Expansion Tank/Extrol Tank
Water expands when it is heated. The expansion tank
creates the extra space needed to hold the heated water.
Thermocouple/Power Pile
Ignition systems that use a pilot flame have a safety control
called a thermocouple. The thermocouple creates a small
electrical current (18
millivolts) that tells the
combination control
that the pilot flame is
burning. If the pilot
flame goes out, the
thermocouple will shut
off the gas supply to the
furnace. A power pile
(750 millivolts) is a more
powerful version of a
Thermocouple/Power Pile
standard thermocouple.
Zone Valve/Dial Valve
Zone valves open and close in
response to the temperature
in the coordinating space.
When a zone calls for heat, the
zone valve opens and trips the
switch that starts the circulator.
The circulator runs until the
Zone Valve/Dial Valve
thermostat is satisfied.
