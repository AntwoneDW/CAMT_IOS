4. Open the access panel on the side of the water heater.
5. Wearing gloves, carefully move the insulation to expose the heating element.
6. Use a multimeter or circuit tester to make sure power is off.
• Touch probes to the terminal screws.
• If the meter display shows power, or if the tester light goes on, the power
is still on.
7. Disconnect the heating element wires and remove the protective collar.
8. Unscrew the heating element with a tongue and groove pliers.
9. Remove the old gasket from around the water heater opening.
10. Coat the replacement gasket with pipe joint compound and place it over
the replacement heating element.
