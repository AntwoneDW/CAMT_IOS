Recovering Refrigerant
Here is the basic procedure for recovering refrigerant.
Safety
Tools and Materials Needed
Wear eye protection.
  • Manifold gauge set
    • Wear sturdy gloves.
  • EPA-approved recovery unit
  • Empty and evacuated recovery
    • cylinder to hold the refrigerant
    recovered
    How-to Steps
    1. Set up your equipment:
    a. Attach the manifold gauge set to the service port. The high-side gauge goes to the high-pressure side; the low-side
    gauge goes to the low-pressure side; the other hose goes to the recovery unit. Be sure the gauge valves are closed.
    b. Connect the recovery cylinder to the recovery unit.
    2. Recover refrigerant from the system, following the manufacturer’s instructions for using the recovery unit. Ensure that you
    get the level of recovery required by the EPA.
    3. Disconnect the instruments and devices.
    For More Information
    If you’d like to explore the topic of recovery in more depth outside of class, refer to Section 4 of your HVAC Servicing
    Procedures manual.
