Electrical Safety
When it comes to electricity, safety can never be compromised. Follow all required
safety procedures when working with electricity. Never take shortcuts, no matter how
busy you may be.
Three Key Dangers
Electricity can cause arc flashes, electrical fires, and electrical shock.
Arc flashes
An arc flash is a short circuit through
the air. In an arc flash, a huge amount of
energy explodes outward from electrical
equipment. The explosion creates
pressure waves that can damage a
person’s hearing, a high-intensity flash
that can damage their eyesight, and
a superheated ball of gas that can
severely burn a worker’s body and melt
metal.
Electrical fires
Fires can be caused by overloaded
electrical receptacles, loose
connections, and melted or corroded
wire insulation.
Electrical shock
Shocks are caused by body contact
with electricity with a high enough
voltage to cause current flow through
muscles and hair.
Work Safely
Fortunately, you can protect yourself and others from electrical dangers by following several simple safety rules.
