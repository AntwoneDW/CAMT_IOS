Fixing a Diverter Valve in a Three-Handle Faucet
Here’s how to remove and clean a diverter valve in a three-handle compression faucet.
Safety
Tools and Materials Needed
Wear hand protection if you use vinegar for cleaning.
  • Replacement diverter cartridge
    • or universal washer kit
  • Screwdriver
  • Adjustable wrench or tongue
    • and groove pliers
  • Ratchet wrench
  • Vinegar
  • Small wire brush
  • Heatproof grease
    • How-to Steps
    1. Shut off the water to the tub faucet.
    2. Remove the diverter valve handle with a screwdriver, then remove the entire diverter knob, also called an escutcheon.
    3. Unscrew (counterclockwise) the bonnet nut with a wrench or pliers.
    4. Unscrew the stem assembly with a ratchet wrench.
    5. Take apart the stem unit.
    6. Replace the stem washer (if needed) with an exact duplicate.
    7. Clean and then grease the stem unit parts. (Replace if parts are worn.)
    8. Put the stem unit back together.
    9. Replace the stem unit.
    10. Attach the diverter knob.
    11. Turn on the water and check operation.
