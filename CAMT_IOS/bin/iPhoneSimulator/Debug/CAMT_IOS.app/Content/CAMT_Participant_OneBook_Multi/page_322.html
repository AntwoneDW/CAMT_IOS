Refrigerants and their Special Properties
Now, let’s lay the groundwork for all the information to come by talking briefly about
refrigerants.
What are Refrigerants?
Refrigerants are chemical compounds that move heat from the inside to the outside of a building. They do this by changing
their state from a liquid to a vapor (or gas)—and back to a liquid again.
More specifically, refrigerants:
  • Absorb heat by evaporating at a low temperature and pressure
  • Expel heat by condensing at a higher temperature and pressure
    • Now that may not sound particularly impressive, but you need to remember that refrigerants begin to boil—that is, change
    from liquid to vapor—at very low temperatures. In fact, the boiling point of a refrigerant can be 40, 50, or 60 degrees below
    zero. So when the air conditioning system turns its liquid refrigerant into a vapor, it can move enormous amounts of heat and
    “cool” the air.
