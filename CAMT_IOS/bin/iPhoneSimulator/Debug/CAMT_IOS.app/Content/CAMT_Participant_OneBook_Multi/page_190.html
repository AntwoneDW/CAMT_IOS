Cleaning a Clogged Showerhead
Showerheads can clog from minerals from hard water and other sediment. When water
comes out of the showerhead erratically, the spray and inlet holes need to be unclogged.
Safety
Tools and Materials Needed
Wear hand protection if you use vinegar for cleaning.
  • Adjustable wrench or tongue
    • and groove pliers
  • Vinegar
  • Small soft bristle brush
  • Paper clip or thin wire
  • Replacement O-ring (if needed)
  • Heatproof grease
    • How-to Steps
    1. Unscrew the showerhead from the
    threaded water pipe with a wrench
    or pliers.
    2. Unscrew the collar nut from the
    showerhead.
    3. Scrub the spray and inlet holes with
    a vinegar-soaked soft bristle brush
    such as a toothbrush.
    4. Clear any stubborn deposits with a
    paper clip or other thin wire.
    5. Rinse the showerhead thoroughly
    with water.
    6. Check the O-ring for signs of wear.
    Replace it if necessary.
    7. Reassemble the showerhead and
    screw it back onto the threaded
    water pipe using Teflon tape.
    8. Turn on the water and check the
    operation.
