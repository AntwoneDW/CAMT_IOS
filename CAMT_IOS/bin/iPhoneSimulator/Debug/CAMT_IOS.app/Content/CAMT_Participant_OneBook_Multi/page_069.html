Electrical Safety (Continued)
Key Safety Rule #3:
Wear Personal Protection
When working with electricity and electrical equipment, wear proper safety protection. This includes:
  • Goggles/eye protection
  • Electrician Gloves
  • Rubber-soled shoes
    • Additionally, if you work with current over 200 amps, your clothes should not contain nylon or elastic, which can conduct
    electricity. Also, never wear jewelry or watches and do not touch metal pipes, faucets, or fixtures. (The metal may create a
    grounding path for electricity to flow through your body.)
    Safety Rule #4: Use Safe Tools and Equipment
    Use tools designed for electrical work such as screwdrivers and pliers with
    insulated handles and fiberglass ladders.
    In addition, use only equipment that is safe and meets electrical codes.
    That means:
  • UL-approved electrical parts and devices.
  • Ground fault circuit interrupters (GFCI) receptacles where specified. They
    • protect against shock and arc flashes.
  • Correct fuses or breakers in the main service panel. Never install a fuse or
    • breaker with a higher amperage rating than the circuit wires.
  • Use only fiberglass ladders when working with electricity.
    • Extension cords should only be used for temporary connections, and should never
    be placed under rugs or fastened to walls, baseboards, or other surfaces.
    Never alter the prongs of a plug to fit a receptacle. Install
    a new grounded receptacle instead.
