Key Parts of a Water Heater (continued)
The Temperature and Pressure Relief Valve (T&P Valve)
All water heaters have a safety device called a temperature and pressure relief valve to let water escape if the water pressure
or temperature exceeds the limits of the tank.
To prevent the potential of scalding, the outlet from the T& P valve should be piped to a safe area.
The Dip Tube
The dip tube is a long narrow tube that directs incoming cold water to the bottom of the tank, preventing it from prematurely
mixing with the outgoing hot water at the top of the tank. Without the dip tube, or with a broken dip tube, it may seem as if
hot water runs out very quickly, or just remains at a lukewarm temperature.
The Anode Rod
The anode rod, sometimes called the sacrificial anode, is a metal rod that prevents the water heater tank from corroding.
Electrolysis eats away the metal anode instead of the metal of the tank.
Bacteria can react with anode rods made of magnesium, which in turn can cause a rotten egg smell in the water. Switching to
an aluminum anode rod can help eliminate this odor.
The Flue
Gas water heaters must be vented to the outdoors, because the combustion of natural gas results in toxic carbon monoxide.
That’s why the center of the tank has a flue, which transports the dangerous gases to the vent mounted on top of the tank.
The vent then carries the carbon monoxide outside the building, keeping both residents and employees safe.
