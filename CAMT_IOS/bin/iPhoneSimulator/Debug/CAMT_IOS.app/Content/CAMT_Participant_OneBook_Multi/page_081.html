How to Measure Resistance
In the simplest terms, resistance means how much the
flow of current in a circuit is impeded. Some resistance is
necessary for heat to be generated in heating elements like
those used in an electric range or electric clothes dryer.
To measure resistance, perform these steps:
1. Turn the power off.
2. Set the multimeter to R X 1.
3. Disconnect any wires.
4. Touch a probe to each of the terminals. The reading can
be away from zero depending on what element is being
measured... For example, a common 6” stove element
can properly read 48 Ohms.
