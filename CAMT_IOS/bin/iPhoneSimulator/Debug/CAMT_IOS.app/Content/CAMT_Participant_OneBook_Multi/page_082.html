Electrical Wires
The word “wired” is often used in conjunction with electricity. In this part of the training,
you’ll survey the world of electrical wires, and then practice working with them.
Wire Materials
Electrical wires are most often made of copper, since it is the best conductor of electricity. Some apartment buildings,
however, have aluminum and copper-clad aluminum wires, which require special repair techniques.
Wire Insulation
Some wires—such as certain grounding wires—may be bare copper. But most
are covered with rubber or plastic vinyl insulation.
  • Rubber was used to insulate wires before 1965.
  • Plastic vinyl has been used since.
    • Color Coding
    Insulation is color-coded to make it easy to identify wire that’s “hot” (carries full
    voltage), neutral (carries zero voltage), or grounded.
  • Hot wires are black, red, or white with black markings.
  • Neutral wires are white or gray.
  • Grounding wires are green or bare copper.
    • Some electrical devices, like ceiling fans, may use another color for the hot wire.
    If you’re not sure which wire is the hot one, use your multimeter.
    Note: A technician should not trust color coding until they verify the colors
    match the purpose of the wire.
