Main Service Panels, Fuses, and Circuit Breakers
The main service panel distributes electrical current to the individual circuits. In apartment
communities, it can most often be found in closets, kitchens, and bathrooms.
Circuit breakers and fuses—inside the main service panel—are safety devices to protect
the electrical system from short circuits and overloads. Let’s learn more.
Circuit Breaker Panels
In apartment buildings, the most common service panel is
a 125-amp circuit breaker panel. The amperage may vary
depending on building code, building age, and demand for
electricity. Amperage ratings range from 15 to 250 amps.
  • Circuit breakers are most common in apartments built
    • after 1965.
  • Single pole circuit breakers protect 120-volt circuits.
  • Double pole circuit breakers protect 240-volt circuits.
  • Ground-fault circuit interrupter (GFCI) and arc-fault
    • circuit interrupter (AFCI) breakers provide protection from
    shocks and fire-causing arcs for the entire circuit.
    Each circuit breaker has a permanent metal strip that heats
    up and bends when voltage passes through it. If a circuit is
    overloaded, the metal strip inside the breaker bends enough
    to “trip” the switch and stop the flow of power.
    Each breaker goes with a different circuit, and circuits should
    be indexed to make them easier to locate.
    Important: Before you make any electrical repair, shut
    off the power to the correct circuit at the main service
    panel, lock and tag it out, and then test the circuit to
    make sure the power is off.
