Clean Removable Burner Tubes and Ports
When carbon and other debris clog the burner tubes and ports, the furnace is unable to
function properly and less heat is generated.
Cleaning the tubes and ports will get the furnace back in tip-top shape.
Safety
Tools and Materials Needed
Make sure the gas and the power to the furnace is shut off.
  • Lockout/tagout device
    • Use lockout/tagout procedures.
  • Screwdriver
    • Wear eye protection.
  • Brush or vacuum
  • Fine wire
    • How-to Steps
    1. Shut off both the gas and the power to the furnace.
    2. Lock and tag out the gas supply line.
    3. Remove the furnace panel.
    4. Unscrew or loosen and remove the burner tubes from the supporting bracket.
    5. Carefully clean the tubes with a brush or vacuum, making sure not to damage the burner ports.
    6. Use a fine stiff wire to clear any debris from clogged ports.
    7. Replace the furnace panel.
