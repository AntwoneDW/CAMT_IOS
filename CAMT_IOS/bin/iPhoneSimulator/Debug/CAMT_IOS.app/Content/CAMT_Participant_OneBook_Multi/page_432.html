Key Parts of an Electric Range
Electric ranges and ovens are generally easy to repair, because there’s not much to go
wrong and there’s not much you can do.
Range Control Panel
The control panel houses the oven thermostat, burner controls, clock, light, and other features.
Cooktop Heating Element
The burner coils that heat up when you turn the electricity on.
Cooktop Receptacle Source
The receptacle source provides electricity to the cooktop heating element.
Oven Baking Element
The heating element coil found on the bottom of the oven chamber.
Oven Broiling Element
The heating element coil located on the top of the oven chamber.
Oven Capillary Tube
The tube attached to the oven wall that senses the temperature in the oven and activates the control that adjusts
the temperature.
Oven Gasket
The gasket is a form-fitting piece that provides a seal around the oven door.
Oven Temperature Control
This adjusts the temperature in the internal chamber when activated by the capillary tube.
Oven Thermostat
Set behind the temperature selector knob, this gauge can be calibrated by adjusting the back of the knob, if the oven actual
temperature is over or under the selected temperature.
