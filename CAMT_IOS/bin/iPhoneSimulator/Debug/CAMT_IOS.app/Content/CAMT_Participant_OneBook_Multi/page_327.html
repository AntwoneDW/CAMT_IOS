What About Window Air Conditioners?
A window (or room) air conditioner has everything contained in one “box.” The units are made small enough to fit into a
standard window frame. You close the window down on the unit, plug the unit in, and turn it on to get cool air.
If you were to take the cover off of an unplugged window unit, you’d find it contains:
  • A compressor
  • A metering device
  • A condenser coil (on the outside)
  • An evaporator coil (on the inside)
  • Two fans (often propelled by a single motor with one fan on each end of the motor)
  • A control unit
    • The fans blow air over the coils to expel heat to the outside and to circulate cooled air around the room.
