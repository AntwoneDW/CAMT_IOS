Troubleshoot an Expansion Tank/Extrol Tank
When a hydronic heating system fails to produce adequate heat, one component to
check is the expansion tank.
Safety
Tools and Materials Needed
Wear eye protection.
  • Hose
    • Wear gloves.
    How-to Steps
    1. Check the pressure-relief valve. If water is spurting from
    it, there is too much water and not enough air in the tank.
    2. Look through the view glass to see how full the
    expansion tank is. If it is more than two-thirds full, drain it
    to the one-half level.
    Note: If an Extrol tank is filled, the air bladder has broken
    and needs replacing. Call a heating service professional.
    3. Return all valves to their normal settings and start the
    boiler.
    4. Check the system’s pressure on the combination
    gauge. If it is not within normal operating range, call for
    professional service.
