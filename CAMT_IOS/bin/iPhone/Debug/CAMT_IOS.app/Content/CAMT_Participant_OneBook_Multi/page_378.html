The Grid:
Air Conditioner Problems and Solutions
Problems
Possible Causes and Solution
The condensing unit does
1. Be sure the thermostat in the apartment is set to “cool” and is at an
not run
appropriate temperature.
2. Check the service panel for a tripped or blown breaker or fuse.
3. Check the disconnect switch. Turn it on if needed.
4. The unit may not be receiving enough power. Use your multimeter to make
sure the unit is receiving the proper input voltage.
5. There may be a continuity problem with the disconnect box fuse. Test the
fuse with your multimeter and replace it if needed.
6. The compressor may be overloaded. Allow it to cool, and then reset it.
7. The compressor or fan motor capacitor may be defective. Discharge the
capacitor, test it, and replace it if necessary.
8. The fan motor may be bad. Test the fan motor and replace it if needed.
9. The compressor motor may be bad. Test the compressor motor and replace
it if needed.
10. The unit’s contactor may be bad.
The condensing unit is noisy
1. The access panel screws may be loose. Tighten them.
2. The condensing unit may not be level. Level it.
3. The fan blades may be hitting the bracket or the grille. Adjust them and
replace them if need be.
4. The fan blades may be bent. Replace them.
5. The fan motor may need oiling. Apply a few drops of oil according to the
instructions on the motor itself.
6. The fan motor may be going bad. Test the motor and replace it if needed.
7. The compressor mounts may be worn or loose. Tighten the mounts or
replace the bushings.
