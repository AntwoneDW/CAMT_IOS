Check the Compressor Motor Start Relay
A relay is a magnetically operated device consisting of coil and one or more sets of
contacts that are used to connect or disconnect the supply voltage circuit to the start
winding of the compressor. Here’s how to test the start relay used with compressors.
Introduction
The compressor motor start relay removes the start capacitor from the motor starting circuit (as determined by the motor
ampere draw), usually equivalent to when the motor reaches 75 to 80 percent of its starting speed.
Motor start relays fail when their contacts remain closed. When this happens, the start capacitor of the motor remains in the
compressor start circuit, causing the start winding to overheat and fail. When the contactors are stuck closed, they may also
cause the start capacitor to fail/
