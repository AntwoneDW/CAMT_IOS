Comparing Electricity and Plumbing (Continued)
Voltage = Pressure
Electricity: Current flows along “pressurized” wires. The “pressure” of an electrical current is called voltage.
Plumbing: Water flows through a pressurized water supply system.
Amperage = Capacity
Electricity: Large wires can carry more electricity than small wires. The rate at which electrical power flows to a light, tool, or
appliance is called amp. The amount of current flowing through a wire is called amperage.
Plumbing: Large pipes can carry more water than small pipes.
