Air Distribution
Air distribution (or circulation) is a basic feature of both gas and electric furnaces. So it’s a
good place to start our discussion of common heating problems and solutions.
The Big Picture
When an apartment thermostat “calls” for warm air, a blower—housed in the furnace cabinet and turned by a motor—draws
air from the return duct, moves the air through a filter to remove dirt and dust, and finally sends the filtered air into the furnace,
where the air is heated.
The heated air then moves into the supply duct and goes out to branch ducts in the building. In some systems, adjustable
dampers in the duct regulate the flow of air to different parts of the building, and individual registers regulate airflow into
a room.
Air from the heated room is pulled through a return air grille and back through the return duct, where the air warming and
distribution cycle starts all over again.
Gas furnaces usually have a fan and limit control mounted on the heat exchanger to switch the blower on and off according
to the temperature of the air.
Electric furnaces usually use blower relays (a set of switches) or fan centers to turn the blower on and off in response to
the thermostat.
In this part of the training, you’ll learn more about the air distribution system, particularly the blower assembly and its
key parts.
