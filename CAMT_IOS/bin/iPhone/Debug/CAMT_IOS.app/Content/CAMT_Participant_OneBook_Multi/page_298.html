Bleed a Hot-water Radiator
When only some radiators are working, the problem could be air trapped within the closed
circuit system. You need to get this air out of the radiator using a process called bleeding.
Safety
Tools and Materials Needed
Wear eye protection.
  • Screwdriver
    • Wear gloves.
  • Bucket
    • How-to Steps
    1. Open the vent with a screwdriver or special tool supplied with the radiator.
    2. When water (instead of air) comes out, close the vent.
    3. If the pressure falls below 12 psi, then add more water.
