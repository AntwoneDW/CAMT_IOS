Air Distribution
Air distribution (or circulation) is a fundamental component of central air conditioning
systems.
Introduction
When an apartment thermostat “calls” for cool air, a blower—housed in the furnace cabinet and turned by a motor—draws
air from the return duct, moves the air through a filter to remove dirt and dust, and finally sends the filtered air into the
evaporator coil, where the heat and moisture is removed from the passing air flow and the is cooled.
The cooled air then moves into the supply duct and goes out to branch ducts in the building. In many systems, adjustable
dampers in the duct regulate the flow of air to different parts of the building, and individual registers regulate airflow into
a room.
Air from the living space is pulled through a return air grille and back through the return duct, where the cycle starts all
over again.
In this part of the training, you’ll learn more about the air distribution system, particularly the blower assembly and its motor.
