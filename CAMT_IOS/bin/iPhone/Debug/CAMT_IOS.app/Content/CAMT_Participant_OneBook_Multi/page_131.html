Replacing a Fluorescent Light Fixture (Continued)
How-to Steps:
1. Turn off the power to the circuit.
2. Remove the shade/diffuser.
3. Remove fluorescent tubes. (Twist the tubes until they
unseat from the sockets.)
4. Remove cover.
5. Use a multimeter or circuit tester to see if the power
has been turned off.
If You Use…
Then Follow These Steps…
A circuit tester
1. Touch one probe of the tester to the green grounding screw.
2. Insert the other probe into each wire connector.
3. Make sure the tester does not light. If it does, there is still power entering
the box.
A multimeter
1. Set the meter to AC volts.
2. Touch one probe of the meter to the green grounding screw.
3. Insert the other probe into each wire connector.
4. Make sure the meter shows 0 to 2 volts. If the number is higher, there is still
power entering the box.
6. Disconnect the wires and ground from the fixture.
7. Unscrew the wire clamp counterclockwise.
8. Unscrew the fixture from the ceiling or wall and carefully remove it.
9. Position new fixture on surface, running wires through the hole in the back of the fixture.
10. Screw/bolt fixture securely in place to studs, if possible.
11. Connect wires and ground to new fixture, using wire connectors. Follow the wiring diagram included with the new fixture.
12. Tighten the clamp holding the wires.
13. Attach cover.
14. Put in tubes and twist until they are seated in sockets at both ends.
15. Put shade/diffuser back on.
16. Restore power to circuit and turn on light to make sure it works.
