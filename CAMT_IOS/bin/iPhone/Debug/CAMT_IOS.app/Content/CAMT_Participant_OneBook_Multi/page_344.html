The Subcooling Method
And now, here’s the subcooling method for charging air conditioning systems.
Introduction
Subcooling is the temperature removed from a refrigerant after it has condensed into a liquid.
You’ll use the subcooling method to check and adjust the charge in air conditioning systems that use thermostatic expansion
valves (TXVs) and newer, environmentally friendly refrigerants such as R-410A.
How It Works
The subcooling method measures the temperature of the refrigerant in the liquid line to determine if the proper quality of
refrigerant is being used in the TXV metering device,
If the liquid line temperature is incorrect, you can change it by adjusting the amount of refrigerant in the system.
To determine the amount of subcooling, you’ll need to test the system using a manifold gauge set and a digital thermometer,
and then do some simple arithmetic.
The Basic Subcooling Formula
Condenser Saturation Temperature - Liquid Line Temperature = System Subcooling Temperature
