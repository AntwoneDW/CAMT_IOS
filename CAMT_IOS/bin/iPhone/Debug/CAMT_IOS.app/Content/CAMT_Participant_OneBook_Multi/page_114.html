Installing a GFCI Receptacle
GFCI receptacles can be wired to protect themselves or to protect all the switches, light
fixtures, and receptacles on a circuit. Here’s how to install a GFCI receptacle to protect a
single location.
Safety
Tools and Materials Needed
Make sure the power is off before you test or replace the receptacle.
  • New GFCI receptacle
    • Use lockout/tagout procedures.
  • Lockout/tagout device
  • Circuit tester or multimeter
    • Use insulated tools.
  • Insulated screwdriver
    • Wear eye protection and rubber-soled shoes. Remove jewelry.
  • Insulated wire stripper or
    • combination tool
  • Wire connectors
    • How-to Steps
    1. Turn off the power to the circuit. Then follow the steps to Lockout/Tagout
    the circuit.
    2. Remove the screws and receptacle cover.
    3. Unscrew the receptacle from the electrical box and—without touching any
    wires—gently pull it out.
    4. Use a multimeter or circuit tester to see if the power has been turned off.
    • Touch one probe to the copper ground wire.
    • Touch the other probe to all three screw terminals.
    • If the light goes on or if the display reads 115 to 124 volts, the power is still
    on and needs to be turned off
    5. Pigtail the white wires together and connect to one WHITE LINE screw terminal.
    6. Pigtail the black wires together and connect to one HOT LINE screw terminal.
    7. Connect grounding wire to grounding screw terminal.
    8. Push switch into electrical box, screw into place, and put on faceplate.
    9. Return power to the circuit and test the receptacle to make sure it works.
    Important: When tightening wires and testing or replacing receptacles,
    be sure power is turned off to that circuit.
