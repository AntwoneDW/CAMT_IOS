Fixing a Leaky Sink Strainer
If water leaks onto the floor or cabinet under a sink, it may be from a leaky sink strainer
assembly. Here’s how to fix it.
Safety
Tools and Materials Needed
Be careful using a putty knife.
  • Replacement sink strainer
    • Wear eye and hand protection.
  • Plumber’s putty or silicone caulk
  • Tongue and groove pliers
  • Spud wrench
  • Hammer
  • Putty knife
    • How-to Steps
    1. Lay down a towel or drop cloth in case of a spill.
    2. Unscrew (counterclockwise) the nuts from the tailpiece of the sink strainer assembly.
    3. Remove the metal or plastic pipe section that connects to the strainer assembly.
    4. Unscrew (counterclockwise) the large diameter nut that secures the assembly to sink.
    5. Remove the sink strainer assembly.
    6. Clean sink and remove putty.
    7. Spread putty in sink drain.
    8. Insert new sink strainer assembly.
    9. Under the sink, install the gasket and ring, and then screw (clockwise) the large diameter nut to secure the assembly
    to sink.
    10. Reinstall the metal or plastic pipe section.
    11. Wipe off the excess putty in sink.
    12. Turn on the water and check for leaks.
