Common Hydronic Heating Problems and Solutions
(continued)
The most common problems with hydronic systems are with the expansion tank or a
circulator rather than the boiler.
Problem
Solution(s)
There is no heat or little heat
1. Be sure the thermostat is set to “heat” and is turned up to an appropriate
temperature.
2. Check the main service panel for a tripped or blown breaker or fuse.
3. Check the water level; it may be too low. Add water as necessary.
4. Check the combination gauge on the boiler to make sure the pressure is in an
acceptable range.
5. Repair any pipe that is leaking.
6. Check the operation of the expansion tank. See page 53 for instructions.
7. Flush rusty water from the boiler. See page 54.
